KESTREL is an sUAS flight trainer. Type a location and fly it in photoreal terrain — on flight physics derived from the real component specifications of your build, not a game approximation.

What makes it real

A trainer, not a tech demo

Every system is grounded in something real — the terrain, the parts, the failure modes. That is the difference between a flight game and a trainer a unit can rely on.

Fly Anywhere

Type a latitude and longitude and load that exact place.

  • Photoreal 3D terrain streamed at real coordinates.
  • The world self-calibrates so you launch on the real ground.

Physics From Real Specs

Mass, motors, prop, and battery come from the actual build.

  • Thrust-to-weight and control feel track the real hardware.
  • Motors are held to their spec-sheet rated point, not fantasy.

Honest Failure

The consequences a real pilot manages.

  • Battery drains, sags, and browns out — the aircraft falls.
  • Fly the link past terrain and it degrades, then fails safe.
  • A crash damages the camera and motors, like it would in the field.

Faithful Controls & OSD

The real flight-controller law.

  • Runs the Betaflight rate and control law — your rates and tune fly the same.
  • A firmware-style on-screen display, on your own radio.

Fly Your Build

Import the loadout you planned in COTS Architect.

  • Real motor, prop, battery, and radio specifications carry over.
  • Fly the kit that is actually on the unit's shelf.

Missions & Records

Objectives, scoring, and a completion record.

  • Structured tasks with pass, timing, and scoring.
  • A saved record for each run — evidence of training completed.

One ecosystem, one build file

Plan it in Architect. Fly it in KESTREL.

A build assembled from a unit's real inventory in COTS Architect exports as a single loadout file. KESTREL flies that exact build on its real specifications — same motors, prop, battery, and radios.

Missions and their scoring records move as plain files, entirely offline. Nothing leaves the operator's computer.

Deployment

A self-contained Windows trainer

No engine install to run, no account, no dependency on a live service for flight. The training loop runs on the operator's own laptop.

Your radio, your build

Fly on a standard RC transmitter in USB mode, with the build imported from COTS Architect. A guided setup maps the sticks and an arm switch.

Photoreal terrain, on demand

Real-world terrain streams at any coordinate. An offline block-terrain fallback keeps the trainer usable without a network.

Grounded, not gamified

Battery, link, and damage behave like the real thing, so habits built in the trainer transfer to the field instead of teaching the wrong reflexes.

Records that travel

Each mission writes a completion record — objectives met, time, and score — as a plain file a unit can keep and review.
