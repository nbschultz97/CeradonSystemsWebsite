FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between a civilian and federal resume?

Federal resumes are typically 3–5 pages (vs 1–2 for civilian), require hours-per-week for each position, supervisor contact info, salary history, and detailed narratives demonstrating specialized experience against specific qualification standards. They also need KSA (Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities) statements matched to the job announcement.

How does FedResume work?

FedResume uses AI to analyze both your civilian resume and the target USAJobs listing. It identifies the position's qualification requirements, KSAs, and specialized experience criteria, then rewrites your experience to match — using proper federal format and GS-appropriate language.

Is $9.99 a one-time fee or subscription?

One-time. Pay once, convert one resume. Apply to multiple similar positions with the same resume, or come back and convert again for a different role at the same price.

Can I use this for military-to-federal transitions?

Absolutely. FedResume handles military experience particularly well — translating MOS descriptions, deployment experience, and military leadership into federal civilian equivalent language that HR specialists recognize.

What GS levels does it support?

FedResume calibrates language for all GS levels from GS-5 through GS-15, as well as SES-equivalent positions. The AI adjusts scope, complexity, and supervisory language to match the target grade.

Is my resume data stored or shared?

Your resume content is processed for conversion only and is not stored after your session ends. We don't share your information with third parties.
