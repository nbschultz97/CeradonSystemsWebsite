Lantern · Alpha

Fly a building. Land. Put the floorplan on the map.

Lantern turns ordinary drone or walkthrough video into a georeferenced interior floorplan and drops it onto ATAK. No survey, no measurements, no ground truth — about two minutes on a laptop.

Request a demonstration See how it works

The flight already happened. The knowledge leaves with it.

A small drone goes through a structure and the operator watches the feed. That feed is good intelligence — right up to the moment it ends. Nothing persists. The next team to look at the same building starts from the same blank satellite tile, and the flight that already happened teaches them nothing.

Lantern makes the flight into a record. The video goes to an edge node on the tactical network — not a cloud service — and comes back as an interior floorplan the operator places on the map. It becomes a durable artifact that outlives the mission it came from.

Video in, floorplan on the map

Every panel below is real output from a single handheld walkthrough of one house — no staging, and no ground truth supplied to the model.

Reconstruct

A dense three-dimensional model is built from the video alone — walls, furniture, fixtures — using no depth sensor and no external reference.

Orient

Which way is up, and which way is north, are solved from the data itself. The camera’s own height and its heading through the entrance resolve the ambiguity without a compass.

Place

The result is written as a georeferenced overlay that imports directly into ATAK and WinTAK. The operator scales and rotates it onto the building footprint.

Built for the map the team already uses

Lantern outputs a georeferenced overlay in the formats ATAK and WinTAK consume natively. The overlay colours itself against the imagery beneath it — light strokes over a dark roof, dark strokes over bright concrete — so it stays readable wherever it lands.

Processing runs on an edge device on the tactical network. The video does not leave the objective, and no connectivity is required.

Where Lantern is today

Lantern is in alpha. We would rather state its limits plainly than have a customer discover them.

Demonstrated

  • Interior reconstruction from ordinary video, with no depth sensor and no supplied reference
  • Orientation recovered from the data alone
  • Georeferenced overlay imported and rendered in WinTAK
  • End to end in roughly two minutes on a single laptop

Not yet

  • Absolute scale is not yet reliable; the operator sets it when placing the overlay
  • Interior proportions currently carry roughly ten percent error against a laser scan of the same building
  • Long featureless spaces such as pipes and tunnels are not recoverable from a single camera
  • Validated on a small number of structures — broader trials are underway

Measured, not asserted

Accuracy is scored against an independent laser scan of the same building. The scan is used for measurement only — an automated check fails the build if any reference geometry reaches the product path.

That constraint exists because a floorplan quietly fitted to a known answer will always look excellent and teach you nothing.
