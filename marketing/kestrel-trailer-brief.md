# KESTREL Launch Trailer Brief

## Goal

Create a 55-second product trailer that proves one workflow:

> Import the equipment a team owns into COTS Architect, configure an aircraft, create a mission, send it to KESTREL, rehearse the objective chain with an RC transmitter, and review the result.

Position KESTREL as an early design-partner mission-rehearsal product for public safety, search and rescue, infrastructure inspection, and professional training teams. It is not a generic FPV game.

Primary message: **Turn the equipment you already own into a mission you can rehearse.**

Closing line: **Plan what you have. Rehearse what you will fly.**

CTA: **Discuss a KESTREL design-partner pilot.**

## Demonstration Scenario

- Aircraft: **Response Quad 7**
- Mission: **Post-Storm Asset Survey**
- Location: a fictional or non-sensitive site with a road, open launch area, and neutral utility or communications asset
- Objectives: launch, ingress gate, stable hover, inspection dwell, return, land, and disarm
- Result: mission complete with objective outcomes, elapsed time or score, and a saved local session summary

Keep the aircraft name, mission name, location, and objective chain consistent in both products. That continuity is the proof.

- Import the fictional XLSX inventory
- Save **Response Quad 7**
- Save the full mission and objective chain
- Use a visible, functional **Send to KESTREL** action

## Capture Checklist

- Record at 2560 x 1440 or 3840 x 2160, preferably 60 fps
- Use a packaged application, not the Unreal Editor
- Disable notifications, desktop overlays, account names, clocks, consoles, and debug labels
- Use deliberate cursor motion and hold important confirmation states long enough to read
- Keep key UI and aircraft action center-safe for 16:9, 1:1, and 9:16 crops
- Record two clean takes of each UI action and three clean mission runs
- Capture the full mission continuously as backup evidence
- Record product audio, voice-over, music, and effects on separate tracks
- Use only fictional inventory and a non-sensitive location
- Verify required terrain and data-source attribution remains visible
- Confirm commercial rights for music, sound effects, fonts, icons, footage, terrain, and models

## Claim Guardrails

Do not say or imply: certified training, qualification, digital twin, exact aircraft replication, identical real-world handling, validated airworthiness, any coordinate on Earth, universal photoreal coverage, fully offline terrain, guaranteed RF or battery fidelity, or unsupported AI capabilities.

Avoid customer data, sensitive locations, weapons, combat, surveillance targets, explosions, fake interfaces, setup terminals, developer overlays, and terrain defects.

Prefer: **location-based rehearsal**, **aircraft-specific configuration**, **structured mission objectives**, **repeatable scoring**, and **local session summary**.
