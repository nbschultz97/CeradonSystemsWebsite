Acoustic Detection

Primary sensor. Always on. USB MEMS mic array captures audio, converts to mel-spectrograms, and runs through a MobileNetV2 CNN for real-time drone classification.

  • ✓ 100–300m detection range
  • ✓ 85–95% classification accuracy
  • ✓ Direction of arrival estimation
  • ✓ Multi-rotor vs fixed-wing discrimination
  • ✓ Known drone fingerprinting (DJI, custom)
  • ✓ Unjammable — works RF-denied

Hardware: $35 mic array

Visual Confirmation

Secondary sensor, cued by acoustic bearing. Gimbal auto-slews to the acoustic detection, YOLO11n identifies and classifies the drone visually.

  • ✓ 50–500m visual range
  • ✓ YOLO11n with drone-specific training
  • ✓ NanoTrack persistent tracking
  • ✓ FLIR Lepton 3.5 thermal (night ops)
  • ✓ Auto slew-to-acoustic-bearing
  • ✓ Day/night auto-switching

Passive RF (Vantage)

Optional third modality via Ceradon’s Vantage platform. Detects drone control link RF signatures without emitting. Also detects ground-level operators.

  • ✓ Zero RF emissions (covert)

How detection works

1

Acoustic Cue

Mic array detects drone audio signature. CNN classifies type. GCC-PHAT estimates bearing. Alert level: YELLOW.

2

Visual Confirm

Gimbal slews to acoustic bearing. Camera acquires visual. YOLO11n confirms drone. NanoTrack locks. Alert level: ORANGE.

3

Fused Track

All sensors correlated. High-confidence classified track pushed to ATAK. Operator alerted. Alert level: RED.
