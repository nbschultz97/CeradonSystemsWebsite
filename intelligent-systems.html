Eight agents. One connected system.

Each agent is purpose-built and connected to your tools. Together they form a loop: content creates leads, leads become sales, sales become deliveries, deliveries become material for more content. The point is not any single agent — it is that they share context instead of living in eight separate tabs.

Content Engine

Turns raw material — recorded calls, notes, briefs — into drafted posts, emails, and articles in your voice, queued for your review before anything publishes.

Scope: drafting · repurposing · scheduling

Sales Manager

Tracks the pipeline, scores opportunities, and summarizes call activity so the state of every deal is visible on one screen instead of living in someone’s head.

Scope: pipeline · scoring · call analysis

Lead Qualifier

Scores and routes every inbound lead against criteria you set. Strong fits get escalated to a human; the rest enter an automated nurture track.

Scope: scoring · routing · escalation

Follow-Up Engine

Runs personalized sequences across email, SMS, and LinkedIn on the cadence you define, so no lead goes quiet because a human forgot.

Scope: sequences · multi-channel · cadence

Operations

Project coordination, meeting notes, task management, and deadline tracking — the administrative layer handled continuously in the background.

Scope: coordination · notes · deadlines

Delivery

Client onboarding, status updates, and milestone tracking, so your clients hear from you at every stage without you writing every message.

Scope: onboarding · updates · milestones

Company Hub

Revenue, pipeline, content, and delivery status on one live dashboard, with flags raised when a number moves in a direction it shouldn’t.

Scope: dashboard · alerts · reporting

Quality Assurance

Checks every piece of client-facing output for voice, accuracy, and compliance before it goes anywhere. Nothing ships on autopilot.

Scope: voice · accuracy · compliance

Frequently asked questions

Won't the content sound like AI wrote it? +

The system trains on your material — your calls, your posts, your way of explaining things — and produces drafts in that voice. A QA agent checks every piece, and you review before anything publishes. The goal is content you would put your name on, not content that slips past detection.

Will this replace my team? +

It is built to replace the hires you have not made yet, not the people you have. The repeatable work — drafting, routing, follow-up, status tracking — moves to the system; your team keeps the judgment calls and gets the hours back.

What if I want to stop? Am I locked in? +

No. You own the agents, the workflows, the dashboards, and the data — it all lives inside your accounts, and you get the documentation at handover. An optimization retainer is available if you want us to keep improving it, but the system does not depend on us to keep running.

How is this different from ChatGPT or Copilot? +

Those are tools; this is infrastructure. We design the architecture first — which agents run, how data flows between them, where automation triggers, what the dashboard tracks — then wire it into the tools you already use. The difference shows up in the connections, not in any single agent.

How much time does my team need to invest? +

A few hours across the install: one recorded session covering your voice and your offer, plus answering questions as we map workflows. We deliberately keep your side small — the build is our job.

What industries does this work for? +

The architecture is workflow-driven, not industry-templated, so the real question is whether your operation runs on repeatable processes — content, lead handling, client delivery, reporting. If it does, it is a candidate. We screen every applicant, and if we do not believe we can deliver, we will tell you on the call.
